TL;DR

Snapper is an open-source iOS client for a backend URL you configure and control. We do not provide financial services, investment advice, brokerage, portfolio management, custody, cryptocurrency exchange, or crypto-asset services. We do not create trading accounts, hold funds or crypto-assets, receive exchange API keys, wallet private keys, or seed phrases, or execute trades on infrastructure controlled by us.

  • No analytics. No third-party SDKs. No telemetry sent to anyone except the backend you configured.
  • No advertising. No ad networks. No cross-site / cross-app tracking.
  • No accounts on our infrastructure. Your account lives on the backend URL you configured.
  • No location data. No microphone / camera / contacts / photos / health access.
  • During normal app use, the only data leaving your device goes to the backend URL you configured and to Apple's APNs servers for push notifications, if you opted in. Apple also processes App Store and TestFlight distribution data separately — see Recipients below.

Legal bases for processing (GDPR Art 6)

  • Authentication credentials and trade-related data — processed on the basis of Art 6(1)(b): necessary to provide the app functionality you requested when configuring and using the app.
  • Device identifier (IDFV) — processed on the basis of Art 6(1)(f): legitimate interest in disambiguating your device registrations so the backend you configured can route alerts correctly. This interest is not overridden by your rights, given that the IDFV is sent only to the backend URL you configured for app functionality and is not used for tracking, advertising, or analytics.
  • APNs device token — processed on the basis of Art 6(1)(a): your explicit consent given when iOS requests notification permission. You may withdraw consent at any time in iOS Settings → Notifications → Snapper.
  • App preferences — processed on the basis of Art 6(1)(b): necessary to restore your UI state between sessions.

Whether providing data is required (GDPR Art 13(2)(e))

  • Authentication credentials and backend requests — not required by law, but necessary to authenticate with and use the backend you configured.
  • APNs device token — optional. If you deny or withdraw notification permission, push alerts will not work, but the rest of the app remains available.
  • Device identifier (IDFV) — not required by law; used for device registration and alert routing. If unavailable, the app uses a fresh random UUID for that registration call.
  • App preferences — not required by law; without them the app may not restore selected UI state between sessions.

Retention periods (GDPR Art 13(2)(a))

  • Authentication tokens and session cookies — retained for the active session. iOS's URLSession cookie storage persists cookies as directed by your backend's Set-Cookie headers; uninstalling the app clears them.
  • App preferences (UserDefaults) — retained until you uninstall the app or reset it via iOS Settings → General → Transfer or Reset iPhone.
  • Device identifier (IDFV) — held in memory for the registration call. iOS resets the IDFV when all apps from this developer are uninstalled. The corresponding device row on your backend is governed by your backend's own retention rules.
  • APNs device token — held in memory for the duration of the app session; relayed to the backend you configured, which controls its own retention. Revoking notification permission in iOS Settings removes the basis for further token relays.

Data the app handles

The app processes the following categories of data on your device so it can do its job. None of this is uploaded to any service operated by us.

Authentication credentials

  • What: username, password (during the login form), and the session tokens / cookies (access_token, refresh_token, ws_token, csrf_token) returned by your backend.
  • How stored: the backend's HTTP session cookies are accepted and replayed by the system URLSession (the app uses URLSession.shared and lets iOS handle cookie storage). The short-lived WebSocket token is held in memory only — never written to disk by the app. The plaintext password is held in memory inside LoginViewModel only for the duration of the login request — LoginViewModel.login() overwrites it with an empty string the moment the login call resolves (success or failure), so it does not persist for the rest of the session.
  • Where it goes: sent to the backend URL you configured over HTTPS during login and refresh requests. Never sent anywhere else.

Trade-related data

  • What: orders, positions, executions, alerts, wallet balances, instrument metadata, and backend-generated analytics or review content. All of this originates from the backend URL you configured and is rendered locally for display.
  • How stored: in-memory only during the app session. Some derived state (selected wallet, time-travel cursor, theme) is persisted to UserDefaults so the app can restore it on next launch.
  • Where it goes: never leaves your device except as instructions or requests you intentionally send to the backend you configured.

No investment advice. Any analytics, alerts, signals, or review content displayed by Snapper are informational outputs from the backend you configured. The iOS app does not generate personalised recommendations, rank assets, or tell you whether to buy, sell, or hold. Mateusz Klatt does not provide investment, financial, tax, legal, or crypto-asset advice through the app.

Push notification token (APNs)

  • What: an opaque device token issued by Apple Push Notification service (APNs) that lets the backend you configured send notifications to this app on this device. APNs also processes the notification payload needed to deliver alerts.
  • When: only after you grant iOS notification permission.
  • How stored: held in memory and relayed to the backend you configured via POST /api/devices. The app does not cache APNs tokens in local storage.
  • Where it goes: to Apple/APNs (operated by Apple Inc., USA) and to the backend you configured. Apple's APNs handling is governed by Apple's privacy policy. Apple states that EEA, UK, and Swiss personal data is controlled by Apple Distribution International Limited (Ireland) and that Apple's international transfers are governed by Standard Contractual Clauses; where Apple's US recipient is certified, the EU-US Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2023/1795) may also be relevant.
  • How to stop: disable notifications in iOS Settings and remove or revoke the device registration on the backend you configured.

Device identifier (IDFV)

  • What: Apple's identifierForVendor — a UUID that identifies your device for this developer (Mateusz Klatt). Different developer = different IDFV.
  • Why: the backend uses it as a per-device key to disambiguate multiple installs, such as iPhone and iPad device rows. See Apple's identifierForVendor docs.
  • Fallback: if iOS returns no identifierForVendor, the app generates a fresh random UUID() for that registration call only. That fallback is not stable across launches.
  • How stored: held in memory and sent to the backend you configured with device-registration calls. The app does not write the IDFV to disk.
  • Where it goes: to the backend you configured only.
  • Deletion / reset: uninstalling Snapper removes its UserDefaults state from the device. iOS resets the IDFV when every app from Mateusz Klatt is uninstalled; if Snapper is installed again later, iOS may provide a new IDFV. The corresponding device row on your backend persists until you delete it through your backend's own interface.
  • Disclosed in PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy as NSPrivacyCollectedDataTypeDeviceID with purpose AppFunctionality and Tracking = false.

App preferences

  • What: UI choices like selected wallet, theme, and time-travel cursor.
  • How stored: Apple UserDefaults (declared in PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy under CA92.1).
  • Where it goes: stays on your device.

Data we do NOT collect

  • The app does not embed any third-party analytics, telemetry, or crash-reporting SDK. Apple may process crash logs as part of App Store and TestFlight distribution; that processing is described under Recipients below.
  • We do not run an ad network or any cross-site / cross-app tracker.
  • We do not access your location, contacts, microphone, camera, photos, or health data. The app does not request any of these permissions.
  • We do not share your data with third parties for advertising or marketing.
  • We do not collect exchange API keys, wallet private keys, seed phrases, or custody credentials.
  • We do not receive, match, route, execute, settle, custody, exchange, or advise on trades on infrastructure controlled by us.
  • We do not maintain any server that holds your Snapper data. Runtime traffic is limited to the backend URL you configured and Apple's APNs if notifications are enabled.

Recipients

The Snapper iOS app communicates with the following recipients beyond your device:

  1. The backend URL you configured (runtime). You configure and control this URL in the app's settings. Account data, session data, trading display data, and device registrations flow here. We cannot access this backend unless you separately give us access.
  2. Apple Push Notification service / APNs (runtime, optional). Used only if you opted in to notifications. APNs receives the device token and the notification payload needed to deliver alerts.
  3. Apple App Store and TestFlight (distribution). Apple processes distribution and beta-testing data outside the runtime app. If you install a TestFlight build, Apple may make tester status, device model, OS version, sessions, crashes, and screenshot or written feedback available in App Store Connect. This is Apple beta-distribution data, not an analytics SDK embedded in Snapper.

There are no other runtime network endpoints. The app does not embed any third-party SDK, analytics library, ad network, or tracking pixel.

Security

The supported backend configuration uses HTTPS for REST requests and WSS for WebSocket connections. iOS App Transport Security applies to URLSession connections by default and requires TLS 1.2 or later unless an explicit ATS exception is present. Do not use a plaintext HTTP or WS backend URL with real data.

Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the following rights with respect to personal data processed by this app (i.e. on-device data and the APNs token relay — not data stored on the backend you configured):

  • Access (Art 15) — request a copy of the data we process.
  • Rectification (Art 16) — correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure (Art 17) — request deletion of your data.
  • Restriction (Art 18) — limit how your data is processed.
  • Portability (Art 20) — receive your data in machine-readable form.
  • Object (Art 21) — object to processing based on legitimate interests; applies to IDFV processing.
  • Withdraw consent (Art 7(3)) — for push notifications, by disabling notifications for Snapper in iOS Settings. Withdrawal does not affect processing carried out before withdrawal.

Because we operate no servers that store your data, most of the above rights are exercised by uninstalling the app (which removes all on-device data) or by revoking notification permission in iOS Settings. For any privacy question contact mateusz@klatt.ie or open a public issue on the GitHub repository.

For data stored on the backend you configured, exercise your rights through that backend. We do not operate that backend and cannot access, correct, export, or delete its data.

Right to lodge a complaint (Art 77). If you believe your GDPR rights have been infringed, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Polish supervisory authority: Urząd Ochrony Danych Osobowych (UODO), ul. Stawki 2, 00-193 Warsaw, Poland — uodo.gov.pl. This right does not affect any judicial remedy available to you under Art 79.
