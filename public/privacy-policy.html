TL;DR

Snapper is a self-hosted trading client. You run your own backend; the app talks to that backend, not to us. This privacy policy describes what the app does on your device and which third parties (Apple, your own backend) the app communicates with.

  • No analytics. No third-party SDKs. No telemetry sent to anyone except your own backend.
  • No advertising. No ad networks. No cross-site / cross-app tracking.
  • No accounts on our infrastructure. Your account lives on your self-hosted backend.
  • No location data. No microphone / camera / contacts / photos / health access.
  • The only data leaving your device goes to your own backend (URL you configured) and to Apple's APNs servers (for push notifications, if you opted in).

Who we are

The Snapper iOS app is open-source software published at github.com/mateusz-klatt/snapper-ios under the MIT license. The app is distributed via the Apple App Store and TestFlight by Mateusz Klatt as the App Store developer of record.

The app is a thin client — it has no server-side component operated by us. All of your data (orders, positions, alerts, credentials) is stored on the backend you (or your operator) run. We have no access to that backend.

Data the app handles

The app processes the following categories of data on your device so it can do its job. None of this is uploaded to any service operated by us.

Authentication credentials

  • What: username, password (during the login form), and the session tokens (access_token, refresh_token, ws_token, csrf_token) returned by your backend.
  • How stored: session tokens are kept in iOS Keychain (encrypted at rest by the OS). The plaintext password is never written to disk; it lives only in the LoginView form state and is dropped after the login request completes.
  • Where it goes: sent to your backend (the URL you configured) over HTTPS during login and refresh requests. Never sent anywhere else.

Trade-related data

  • What: orders, positions, executions, alerts, wallet balances, instrument metadata, AI review payloads. All of this originates from your backend and is rendered locally for display.
  • How stored: in-memory only during the app session. Some derived state (selected wallet, time-travel cursor, theme) is persisted to UserDefaults so the app can restore it on next launch.
  • Where it goes: never leaves your device except as part of the trading commands you submit back to your own backend.

Push notification token (APNs)

  • What: an opaque identifier issued by Apple Push Notification service (APNs) that lets your backend send notifications to your device. Generated by iOS only after you grant the notification permission.
  • How stored: held in memory + relayed to your backend via POST /api/devices so your backend knows where to send alerts.
  • Where it goes: to Apple (APNs is an Apple service) and to your own backend. Apple's APNs handling is governed by Apple's privacy policy. Your backend can revoke or re-register your device at any time.

Device identifier (IDFV)

  • What: Apple's identifierForVendor — a UUID that identifies your device for this developer (Mateusz Klatt). Different developer = different IDFV. Resets if you uninstall every app from this developer.
  • Why: the backend uses it as a stable per-device key to disambiguate multiple installs (e.g. iPhone + iPad would be two separate device rows). This is the standard Apple-recommended privacy-friendly device identifier — see Apple's "Identifying a device" docs.
  • How stored: held in memory + sent to your backend with each device-registration call.
  • Where it goes: to your own backend only.
  • Disclosed in PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy as NSPrivacyCollectedDataTypeDeviceID with purpose AppFunctionality and Tracking = false.

App preferences

  • What: UI choices like selected wallet, theme, and time-travel cursor.
  • How stored: Apple UserDefaults (declared in PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy under CA92.1).
  • Where it goes: stays on your device.

Data we do NOT collect

  • We do not run any analytics, crash reporting, or telemetry SDK.
  • We do not run an ad network or any cross-site / cross-app tracker.
  • We do not access your location, contacts, microphone, camera, photos, or health data. The app does not request any of these permissions.
  • We do not share your data with third parties for advertising or marketing.
  • We do not maintain any server that holds your data — the only servers in the loop are your own backend and Apple's APNs.

Third parties

The app communicates with exactly two parties beyond your device:

  1. Your own backend. You (or your operator) configure the URL in the app's settings. All trading data flows here. Snapper is open source — you can read the wire protocol at github.com/mateusz-klatt/snapper-ios.
  2. Apple Push Notification service (APNs). Used only if you opted in to notifications. Subject to Apple's privacy policy.

There are no other network endpoints. The app does not embed any third-party SDK, analytics library, ad network, or tracking pixel.

Children's privacy

The app is rated 17+ on the App Store and is not intended for children under 17. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Your rights

Because we do not operate any servers that hold your data, requests for access, correction, deletion, or portability of your data should be directed to your backend operator (the operator of the backend URL you configured in the app).

If you have privacy questions about the app's on-device behavior or its source code, contact us via the email below or open a public issue on the GitHub repository.

Changes to this policy

Material changes will be announced via:

  • An updated "Last updated" date at the top of this document.
  • A new commit to the source repository with a clear changelog entry.
  • For App Store-relevant changes, an updated "Privacy Policy URL" in App Store Connect.

There is no in-app notification mechanism for privacy policy changes — the change history is the git log of the source repository.
